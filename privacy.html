Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 2026

This policy explains what data VoxFlow collects, what it does with it, and what it does not do. It is written to be read, not skimmed.

The short version

VoxFlow does not collect your data. Your audio never leaves your machine. Your transcriptions are stored locally on your own PC and are never transmitted anywhere. There are no analytics, no tracking, and no third-party data sharing built into the application.

The only external network contact VoxFlow makes is checking a version number file on GitHub to see if an update is available. That request contains no personal information.

1. Who This Policy Covers

This Privacy Policy applies to users of the VoxFlow Windows desktop application, developed and maintained by JH Dev. It also applies to visitors of this website.

2. Data Collected by the Application

Audio recordings: VoxFlow captures audio from your microphone while you are actively recording. This audio is processed locally by the Whisper AI model on your own machine. It is never saved to disk and never transmitted to any server.

Transcriptions: Your transcribed text is stored locally in a settings file on your PC at %AppData%\VoxFlow\settings.json. This includes your recording history, custom vocabulary, and text templates. This file exists entirely on your machine and is never sent anywhere.

Application settings: Your preferences including hotkey configuration, language selection, and display options are stored in the same local settings file. This data never leaves your device.

To be explicit: VoxFlow has no backend server. There is no database storing your information. There is no account system. Nothing you say or type through VoxFlow is accessible to anyone but you.

3. Network Requests Made by the Application

VoxFlow makes exactly two types of outbound network requests:

  • Model download: On first launch, VoxFlow downloads the Whisper AI model file (~140MB) from a public GitHub release. This is a one-time download and contains no personal information.
  • Update check: VoxFlow periodically fetches a plain text file from GitHub containing the latest version number. This request contains no personal data — it is equivalent to visiting a webpage.

No other network requests are made by the application under any circumstances.

4. Data Collected by This Website

This website is hosted on GitHub Pages. GitHub may collect standard web server logs including IP addresses and browser information as part of their standard hosting infrastructure. This is outside my control and subject to GitHub's Privacy Statement.

This website does not use cookies, analytics trackers, or any third-party tracking scripts beyond what is inherent to GitHub Pages hosting.

5. Purchase Data

VoxFlow is sold through Gumroad. When you make a purchase, Gumroad collects and processes your payment information. I receive only what Gumroad shares with sellers, typically your email address for purchase confirmation purposes.

Your payment card details are never seen or stored by me. All payment processing is handled entirely by Gumroad and subject to Gumroad's Privacy Policy.

6. Bug Reports

If you use the built-in bug reporter in VoxFlow (Settings → About), it opens an email draft in your browser pre-filled with basic system information including your OS version, microphone name, and current app settings. This information is sent only if you choose to send the email, and only to my direct email address.

I use this information solely to diagnose and fix technical issues. It is not shared with third parties.

7. Children's Privacy

VoxFlow is not directed at children under the age of 13. I do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information through a bug report or direct email, please contact me and I will delete it promptly.

8. Your Rights

Because VoxFlow does not collect or store your personal data on any server, most traditional data rights requests do not apply in the usual sense. However:

  • You can delete all locally stored VoxFlow data at any time by deleting the folder at %AppData%\VoxFlow
  • You can uninstall VoxFlow at any time through Windows Settings or Control Panel
  • If you have contacted me via email and want that correspondence deleted, email me and I will remove it

9. Changes to This Policy

If this privacy policy changes in a meaningful way — for example if VoxFlow ever added any form of data collection — I will update this page and note the change in the application's update changelog.

The current version of this policy will always be available at this URL.
